The story

This definitely wasn't the plan.

I was supposed to be a psychologist. Degree? Done. Higher studies abroad? Planned. Life? Sorted.

Then COVID happened and, like most plans in 2020, mine quietly packed its bags and left. While I was home trying to figure out what came next, my family offered me a deal: "Learn something useful and we'll get you a laptop and a new phone."

Now, I wish I could tell you I had some grand vision for my future. I didn't. I simply wanted the laptop. So I signed up for a graphic design course.

One course became a job. One job became a career nobody saw coming — least of all me.

What was supposed to be a temporary way to spend a few months somehow turned into a career. I found myself working on content, branding, strategy, websites, marketing, client management and operations. I went from designing posts to sitting in rooms where real business decisions were being made.

Over the years, I had a front-row seat to businesses of all shapes and sizes. I worked closely with founders, watched brands grow, learned how teams function, and discovered what actually happens behind the scenes of successful businesses.

And somewhere in the middle of all that, I realised something. I may not have become a psychologist. But I never stopped studying people. Today, I just do it through brands — through the stories they tell, the way they communicate, the way people connect with them, and the reasons customers choose them.

Great marketing isn't really about algorithms, trends, or posting every day. It's about understanding people.

Funny how I ended up using my psychology degree after all.

Eventually, there came a point where I stopped asking, "Could I do this on my own?" And started asking, "Why not?" So I did.

Now I help brands find their voice, tell better stories, and build a presence that feels less like marketing and more like a genuine connection.

What guides the work

A few things I genuinely believe.

People over algorithms

Trends change weekly. Human psychology doesn't. I build for the people behind the metrics.

Details are the brand

The font, the spacing, the tone of one caption. Quality lives in decisions most people never notice.

Collaboration, not handover

You know your business. I know how to tell its story. The best work happens when both of us show up.

That's the short version

The long version involves a lot of coffee.

Several existential crises about font pairing, and a genuine belief that every brand has a story worth telling properly. If yours is ready to be told, I'd love to hear it.
